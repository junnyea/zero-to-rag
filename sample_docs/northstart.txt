Cybersecurity Incident SummaryOn 12 March 2026, an employee received a phishing email that appeared to come from the Finance Director.The email requested an urgent supplier-bank-account change.The employee reported the email to the Security Operations team before making the requested change.The investigation confirmed that no credentials were entered and no payment was made.The malicious sender domain was blocked.The company introduced an additional verbal-verification requirement for supplier bank-account changes.Information Classification: ConfidentialAllowed Groups: Information Security, Finance, Internal Audit, Executive Management15. Supplier Bank-Account Change ProcedureAll supplier bank-account changes must follow this procedure:1. The supplier submits the request through the approved supplier portal.2. Procurement validates the supplier identity.3. Finance independently contacts the supplier using a previously verified telephone number.4. A second Finance employee reviews the request.5. The Finance Manager approves the change.6. The system records the requester, reviewer, approver and approval time.Bank-account changes must not be approved using only information contained in an email.Urgency is not a valid reason to bypass verification.Information Classification: ConfidentialAllowed Groups: Procurement, Finance, Internal Audit16.
